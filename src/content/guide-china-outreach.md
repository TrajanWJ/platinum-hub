## Why US/retail ghosted you (and China won't)
- US retailers/print shops ignore small first orders — no upside for them.
- Alibaba OEMs live on inquiries and **chase serious buyers**. The trick is to look serious and specific.
- Biggest unlock: **post an Alibaba RFQ (Request for Quotation)** — suppliers come to YOU and compete. Pair that

## What gets a fast, good reply
- **Be specific** (the spec list above) — vague "how much?" gets ignored.
- **Name quantities + reorders** — signals real, repeat business.
- **Ask for a sample** — separates buyers from tire-kickers.
- **Use Trade Assurance** for the order (Alibaba escrow) — protects your money; also filters to legit suppliers.
- Message **5–8 suppliers at once**; reply rate is a numbers game. Expect answers within 24–48h (China hours).

## Vetting a reply before you pay
- Verified/Assessed/Gold supplier + years active; ask for a **business license**.
- Get a **sample first**, then stress-test: 50 flip cycles, remote range, a water spray, does it hold position
  with power off. Only then place the trial order.
- Confirm in writing: metal frame, IP rating, included remote + FCC, custom-logo method, MOQ, unit price tiers,
  lead time, Trade Assurance. Put it in the PO.

## Negotiation notes
- First quote is rarely the floor — ask for price at the next tier up and a sample-cost credit on first order.
- Tooling/logo setup fee is often waived or refunded against a trial order — ask.
- A sourcing agent (Yiwu/Guangzhou) can help if comms stall, but isn't needed to start.

## Legal (unchanged)
Market show/off-road/export only; don't ship to DE/TN/FL; remote should carry an FCC ID so the unit is labelable.
