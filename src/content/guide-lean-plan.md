## Flippers — the IN-BETWEEN solution (OEM platform, you own the quality)
The reliability problems aren't the whole unit — they're 3 specific things: a cheap motor that
back-drives/floods, a fixed-code remote that fails or gets cloned, and zero QC. Fix exactly those.
This sits between "rebrand junk" (too cheap) and "design from scratch" (too much). No CAD, no US fab.

1. **Source from an AUDITED OEM/ODM, not the $25 cheapest.** On Alibaba filter to Verified/Assessed
   suppliers tagged OEM/ODM/self-patent (~23 exist). **Spec these as requirements:**
   - metal frame (aluminum/stainless), NOT ABS plastic
   - **IP67 waterproof motor** + **self-locking** mechanism (holds position powered off, no droop)
   - custom logo on the frame (they all do OEM branding)
   - Targets from research: Changzhou Yueze (aluminum, ISO9001), Shanghai Fa Yu (ISO9001, custom logo),
     Shenzhen Reliant (MOQ 2). Reference for "what good looks/costs like": PlateVanish, 510 Auto Group.
2. **Standardize the remote — the one electronics upgrade worth doing.** Replace the stock fob with a
   **Solidremote** rolling-code FCC fob (Amazon): better range, can't be cloned, FCC-legit. ~5–10 min wiring.
3. **On assembly add the cheap reliability stuff:** dielectric grease on connectors, gasket/sealant on the
   motor seam, stainless + thread-locked fasteners.
4. **Test EVERY unit (this is your real differentiator):** ~30 flip cycles + remote range check + a water
   spray. Log it. Offer a 6–12 month warranty — cheap sellers can't, because they don't test.

Result: ~15–20 min/unit, genuinely reliable + "custom," no factory needed.

**Sample first:** order 2–3 sample units from 2–3 OEMs, run them HARD, pick the best platform. Judge a sample by:
metal frame · motor holds position with power off · survives a water spray · 50 clean flips · remote 30+ ft.
- **PARK** (only when ~100+/mo): custom CAD frame + US fab (SendCutSend) + contract sub-assembly.

## Plates (lazy version)
1. **Buy sublimation-COATED aluminum blanks** (.032", 6×12) — Unisub, JPPlus, or JDS. The *coating* is what stops the rub-off; bare aluminum is why the last batch failed.
2. **Send to ONE print shop** that takes your blanks + sublimates (or UV-prints + laminates):
   - **Uptown Press**, Baltimore (closest, 410-889-8686), or **DPI Cherry Hill**, NJ (drivable).
3. **Test ONE sample before the batch:** scratch it with a coin + rub on concrete. Survives = order the batch.
- **PARK:** reflective real-plate builds, dropship/POD, comparing 5 suppliers.

2. **Always abrasion-test a plate sample before a bulk plate order.**
