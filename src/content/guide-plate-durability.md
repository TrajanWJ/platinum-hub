# Sourcing — Custom Plates (own blanks → print shop, abrasion-proof)

Last updated: 2026-06-19. Scope: US (DC/DMV). Model: **owner sources own blanks, ships to a print shop** (tolling).
**#1 requirement: print must NOT rub off** — last batch failed on rocks/abrasion. Tags: [unverified] where noted.

## Root cause of the rub-off (plain answer)
The last batch was **bare ink on bare metal with no topcoat** — either (a) UV/digital ink printed straight onto
uncoated aluminum with no adhesion primer and no clear laminate (most likely), or (b) sublimation attempted on
**non-coated** blanks (dye only bonds into a polyester coating; on raw aluminum it sits on top and wipes off).
Fix is non-negotiable: **a bonding layer underneath (poly-coat or primer) AND/OR a hard clear layer on top
(overlaminate film / clearcoat / overlay).** Never ship ink straight on metal.

## Durability ranking (road-debris abrasion, best→worst)
1. Powder coat — toughest, but solid-color only (good for base/border, not full art).
2. **Reflective sheeting + clear overlay** — how real plates are made (3M/Avery); image under a hard film. Very durable.
3. **Dye-sublimation on poly-coated aluminum** — color infused IN the coating, can't rub off. Best for full-color art + lowest fabrication risk.
4. Baked enamel — durable but brittle.
5. **UV ink + primer + clear overlaminate/hardcoat** — good when both layers present; works on any substrate incl. acrylic.
6. Screen/UV ink, no topcoat.
7. **Bare UV ink on uncoated aluminum, no primer/topcoat ← last batch.**

## Recommended method + exact blank to source
**Primary — Dye-sublimation.** Source **sublimation-coated ("sublimation-ready"/poly-coated) aluminum, 6"×12"**
(Unisub is 5.88×11.88 to fit frames), **gauge .030–.032"** for real-plate heft (.025" is the volume standard but feels
thinner; legal range .022–.032"). **Do NOT buy bare/mill aluminum for sublimation** — it must say sublimatable/poly-coated.
**Alternative — UV + laminate.** Any flat aluminum (.032") or acrylic; require the printer to (a) apply a metal/acrylic
**adhesion primer** before printing and (b) apply a **clear abrasion-resistant overlaminate or hardcoat** on top.

## Next steps (PROVE durability before any bulk)
1. Pick lane: **dye-sub on coated blanks** (recommended) or **UV + primer + laminate** (if raw/acrylic).
2. Order 25–50 sample blanks (.030–.032" 6×12) from Unisub/JPPlus/JDS.
3. Get sample runs from 2 shops first — lead **ASAP Reprographics** + **DPI Cherry Hill**. Ask in writing: "print on customer-supplied 6×12 blanks? apply clear abrasion-resistant laminate/topcoat?"
4. **Abrasion test every sample before bulk:** coin/key scratch ×20–30, rub test (dry + isopropyl cloth ×50), cross-hatch tape pull, rub on concrete/sandpaper. Compare sublimated vs UV+laminate vs bare-UV to see last batch's failure mode.
5. Confirm gauge feel — spec **.032"** if .025" feels flimsy.
