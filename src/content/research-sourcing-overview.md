# Platinum Plates — Sourcing Overview: Three Paths to a Custom Motorized Plate Flipper

**Brand context:** Platinum Plates (Washington DC / DMV), 2-person social-first brand. Goal: sell a high-quality, reliable, custom-branded motorized license-plate flipper for US plates (6"x12", single & double), positioned strictly for **show / off-road / private-property / export** use.

**Read this first:** Motorized flippers are road-illegal in the US, and a growing number of states ban the *sale and manufacture* itself — not just road use. The legal section at the bottom is not boilerplate; it shapes which path and which states you can sell into. See [§ US Legal & Compliance](#us-legal--compliance).

---

## The Three Paths (Balanced)

There are three realistic ways to put a branded flipper into market. None is strictly "best" — each trades cost, speed, control, and risk differently.

| | **Path A — Buy whole OEM unit & rebrand** | **Path B — OEM platform + upgrades** | **Path C — Full custom design + US fab** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **What it is** | Source a finished flipper from a China OEM/ODM (Alibaba/Global Sources) or a US reseller's unit; add your logo/packaging. | Start from an OEM base unit, but spec upgrades: metal frame, IP67 self-locking motor, FCC-certified remote module, your branding molded/etched in. | Design your own mechanism + frame from scratch; fabricate frame/assembly domestically (US machine shop), source motor/remote as components. |
| **Upfront cost** | Lowest. Sample $30–80/unit China FOB [unverified — quote-dependent]; US-finished units retail ~$225 ([510 Auto Group](https://www.510autogroup.com/products/stealth-plate-flippers-usa)). | Medium. Tooling/branding setup + higher per-unit; MOQ often rises with customization. | Highest. CAD, prototyping, tooling, US machining rates, FCC module integration testing. |
| **MOQ reality** | Low/feasible. Some China suppliers list **5-piece MOQ** (e.g. Guangzhou Huyi via [Accio](https://www.accio.com/plp/license-plate-flipper-car)); samples of 1–5 common. | Custom branding usually pushes MOQ up (molded logo needs tooling). Etched/printed branding can stay low. | N/A — you set it, but per-unit only makes sense at volume. |
| **Time to market** | Fast. 7–15 day lead times quoted on Alibaba for stock units ([Accio](https://www.accio.com/plp/license-plate-flipper-car)). | Medium. Add weeks for tooling/sample iteration. | Slow. Months: design → prototype → test → fab. |
| **Quality control** | You inherit the OEM's QC. Variable; ABS-heavy budget units common. Must vet samples hard. | Better — you can demand metal frame, IP rating, self-lock in the spec and reject off-spec lots. | Highest control, but you own every defect and warranty claim. |
| **Branding depth** | Surface only (sticker/laser-etch + your packaging). Hard to claim "designed by us." | Real — molded/etched logo, custom remote shell, branded box. Defensible "our product." | Total — proprietary mechanism, design IP, patentable in principle. |
| **Differentiation** | Low — same unit your competitors dropship. | Medium-high — visibly better build than the dropship pack. | High — genuinely your own product. |
| **Risk** | Race-to-bottom pricing; you're one of many reselling the identical SKU. | Supplier dependency; tooling sunk cost if you switch factories. | Capital risk, long runway, you carry all warranty + FCC liability. |
| **Fits a 2-person social brand?** | Yes, to start. | Yes — the natural step-up once you have demand signal. | Not yet — premature for a 2-person brand. |

## What We're Looking For — The SPEC (Criteria Checklist)

Use this as the hard filter when evaluating any supplier or unit. Items marked **(must)** are non-negotiable for the Platinum Plates positioning; **(plus)** are tie-breakers.

- [ ] **Frame: metal (aluminum or stainless), NOT cheap ABS** *(must)* — ABS is the budget tell; many Alibaba units and even some US-finished units use "stainless steel with high-end ABS components" ([PFYC Show-N-Go](https://www.pfyc.com/show-n-go-retractable-license-plate-frame)). Confirm load-bearing parts are metal, not just the trim.
- [ ] **Motor: IP67 / genuinely waterproof** *(must)* — IP67 is claimed by some China suppliers ([Accio](https://www.accio.com/plp/license-plate-flipper-car)); US resellers often only say "anti-rust, withstands rain/snow" with **no IP rating** ([510 Auto Group](https://www.510autogroup.com/products/stealth-plate-flippers-usa)). Get the rating in writing.
- [ ] **Self-locking mechanism — holds position with power off, no droop** *(must)* — worm-gear/self-locking drive is the quality marker. Many cheap units rely only on motor torque and sag over time. Often **not stated**; ask explicitly and test the sample by cutting power mid-position.
- [ ] **Fits US plates: 6"x12" (305×152mm), single & double config** *(must)* — confirmed achievable; 510's unit fits 12"×6" with single and double kits.
- [ ] **Custom logo / OEM branding capability** *(must)* — OEM/ODM branding is "a key competitive edge" among B2B suppliers ([Accio](https://www.accio.com/plp/license-plate-flipper-car)); Alibaba lists 6 OEM / 5 ODM / 5 self-patent factories ([Alibaba](https://www.alibaba.com/license-plate-flipper-suppliers.html)).
- [ ] **Low MOQ (sample/MOQ 1–20) OR real dealer/wholesale program** *(must)* — 5-piece MOQ exists (Guangzhou Huyi); US resellers like 510 Auto Group / Flipper House sell single units but MOQ/wholesale terms are **not published** — must ask.
- [ ] **Ships to the USA** *(must)* — China FOB + your import, or a US-domiciled reseller/dropship.
- [ ] **Anti-theft locking** *(plus)* — locking systems cited as a durability/trust differentiator ([Accio](https://www.accio.com/plp/license-plate-flipper-car)).
- [ ] **Rolling-code / FCC-certified remote** *(plus, becomes a must if sold in the US)* — see legal section; the practical route is a **module that already carries an FCC ID** ([ib-lenhardt modular approval](https://ib-lenhardt.com/kb/fcc-requirements/modular-approval)).
- [ ] **Warranty ≥ 1 year** *(plus)* — 510 Auto Group offers a 1-year limited warranty; treat that as the floor.
- [ ] **Long-term relationship signal** *(plus)* — responsive sample process, willingness to iterate on spec, consistent factory (not a trading-company middleman).

## Quality Scorecard Rubric

Score every candidate unit 1–5 per row. A unit must clear the weighted bar before it carries the Platinum Plates name. Anything scoring 1 on a **must** row (frame, IP, self-lock) is an automatic reject regardless of total.

| Criterion | Weight | 1 (reject) | 3 (acceptable) | 5 (premium) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Frame material** | ×3 | Mostly ABS plastic | Stainless trim + ABS core | Solid aluminum or stainless load-bearing frame |
| **IP / waterproofing** | ×3 | No rating; "splash resistant" | Vague "waterproof," IP-unspecified | Documented **IP67** with test report |
| **Self-locking / position hold** | ×3 | Sags when power cut | Holds short-term, some creep | Worm-gear self-lock, holds indefinitely power-off |
| **Remote type & compliance** | ×2 | Unknown RF, no FCC ID | Basic fixed-code remote | Rolling-code, module with **FCC ID** |
| **QC & build consistency** | ×2 | No sample/inspection offered | Sample provided, minor lot variance | Sample + factory QC docs, consistent lots |
| **Warranty & support** | ×1 | None | 1-year limited | ≥1-year + responsive replacement parts |
| **Branding capability** | ×1 | Sticker only | Laser-etch / printed logo | Molded logo + custom remote shell + box |

**Verification beats vendor claims.** Always buy 1–2 samples and test: power-off droop, water spray, remote range/range-under-obstruction, cycle count. Vendor IP67/self-lock claims are routinely overstated.

## US Legal & Compliance

This is the section that constrains the business. **Read it before committing to a path or a target market.**

### 1. Road-illegal everywhere; positioning must be show/off-road/export only
Operating a vehicle on a public road with an obscured/flippable plate is illegal across US jurisdictions. Legitimate sellers position units as **show / off-road / track / private-property / export only** — e.g. 510 Auto Group labels its unit "Off-Road use only, such as car shows, track events, and photo shoots" ([510 Auto Group](https://www.510autogroup.com/products/stealth-plate-flippers-usa)). Platinum Plates must carry the same disclaimer prominently on the product, listing, and packaging.

### 2. A growing list of states ban the SALE and MANUFACTURE itself
This is the critical shift: several states now criminalize manufacturing, selling, offering to sell, distributing, or possessing the device — not just using it on the road. As of late 2025, **eight states** have acted ([Land Line Media, "new laws in eight states"](https://landline.media/new-laws-aim-to-stop-drivers-dodging-tolls-and-cameras/)):

| State | What's banned | Penalty (as reported) |
|---|---|---|
| **Delaware** | Manufacture, sell, offer to sell, purchase, possess, install, operate, distribute | 30–90 days jail and/or $50–$200 first offense; up to 6 mo / $300 repeat ([Land Line](https://landline.media/more-states-take-action-to-address-license-plate-flippers/)) |
| **Tennessee** (since 7/1/2024) | Manufacture, purchase, sale, possession | Up to 1 yr jail and/or $2,500 ([Land Line](https://landline.media/states-take-steps-to-address-concern-about-license-plate-flippers/)) |
| **Florida** | Manufacture, purchase, possess, sell, distribute (misdemeanor); felony if used to evade toll | Up to 1 yr / $1,000; 3rd-degree felony up to 5 yr / $5,000 ([Land Line](https://landline.media/new-laws-aim-to-stop-drivers-dodging-tolls-and-cameras/)) |
| **Texas** (since 2013) | Device banned | per state statute |
| **Washington** (since 2013) | Device banned | 364 days jail + $1,000 first offense ([Land Line](https://landline.media/new-laws-aim-to-stop-drivers-dodging-tolls-and-cameras/)) |
| **Pennsylvania** | Use of flippers forbidden ([NBC Philadelphia](https://www.nbcphiladelphia.com/news/local/license-plate-flipping-devices-banned-in-pa/4033579/)) | per state statute |
| **California** | AB1085 bans tinted/shaded/flipper-style plate covers [scope still firming up — verify] | per bill |
| **Illinois** (action begun Apr 2025) | Moving to ban the devices | per pending law |

**Implication for Platinum Plates:** Do **not** ship/sell into states that ban sale/possession (at minimum DE, TN, FL, TX, WA). Geo-block those states in the storefront and shipping rules. Treat CA, PA, IL as ban or pending-ban. **Verify each state statute directly before launch** — this list is moving fast and the above is press reporting, not legal advice. *[Specific statute citations not pulled in this pass — confirm with counsel.]*

### 3. DC / DMV note (home market)
Platinum Plates is DC-based. **Confirm DC, Maryland, and Virginia statutes specifically** before selling locally — the DMV is exactly the kind of toll-camera-heavy market that draws flipper-ban legislation. Do not assume the home market is open. [unverified — verify DC/MD/VA code directly]

### 4. FCC compliance for the remote (RF device)
A wireless remote is an intentional radiator and needs FCC authorization to be marketed in the US. The only practical route for a 2-person brand: **integrate a radio module that already holds its own FCC ID** (modular approval). Then the host product carries a label such as *"Contains Transmitter Module FCC ID: XXXXXXXXX"* ([ib-lenhardt](https://ib-lenhardt.com/kb/fcc-requirements/modular-approval); [FCC KDB 996369 module integration guide](https://apps.fcc.gov/kdb/GetAttachment.html?id=bNCiEdkFEKnHsZF9GHCNdg%3D%3D)). You must still follow the module's integration guidance and may need limited verification testing if you change antenna/firmware/RF parameters. A no-name China remote with no FCC ID is a compliance liability — reject it. Rolling-code is a security/anti-clone plus on top of, not a substitute for, FCC authorization.

### 5. Export framing
"Export only" is a legitimate channel but carries its own rules (destination-country road laws, export documentation). It does not exempt you from US sale/manufacture bans in ban states if the device is made/sold/possessed there.

*Nothing here is legal advice. Before launch, have counsel confirm: (a) device sale/possession legality per target state, (b) DC/MD/VA home-market status, (c) FCC labeling on the final unit, (d) the off-road disclaimer language.*

## Recommendation — for a 2-person social-first brand

**Start on Path A, structured to graduate to Path B. Skip Path C for now.**

1. **Sample-first, Path A.** Order 2–3 samples from low-MOQ China OEM/ODM factories (the 5-piece-MOQ tier) **and** one US-finished unit (e.g. 510 Auto Group at $225) as a quality/price benchmark. Run every sample through the scorecard — especially the power-off droop and water tests, which is where budget units fail.
2. **Pick the supplier on the must-rows, not price.** Metal frame + documented IP67 + real self-lock are the three that separate a returnable, on-brand product from a dropship commodity. If a factory can't evidence all three, it's a Path A volume seller, not your supplier.
3. **Graduate to Path B as soon as you have demand signal.** Once samples sell, commit to one factory for molded/etched branding, a branded remote shell, and a spec sheet you control. This is where "Platinum Plates" becomes a real product instead of a relabeled SKU — and it keeps MOQ/capital sane for two people.
4. **Insist on an FCC-ID'd remote module from day one.** It's the cheapest compliance insurance you'll buy and it's much harder to retrofit later.
5. **Lock the legal posture before the first sale.** Off-road/show/export disclaimer everywhere; geo-block ban states (DE, TN, FL, TX, WA, and treat CA/PA/IL as closed); confirm DC/MD/VA home-market status with counsel. The legal map, not the product, is the real constraint on this business.

**Why not Path C:** full custom design + US fabrication means capital, months of runway, FCC liability you own end-to-end, and warranty exposure across every defect — all premature for a 2-person brand still validating demand. Revisit it only if the brand scales and you want defensible product IP.
